# Wikipedia Merge/Split Rules — Reference

## When to Merge (from Wikipedia:Merging)
- Two articles cover the same topic under different names
- One article is a small subtopic that can't be expanded and is better as a section
- >50% of content would be duplicated between two pages
- Topic doesn't have enough depth for a standalone article

## When to Keep Separate
- Both topics are independently notable (can stand alone)
- Combined article would be too long (>50KB)
- Topics are related but fundamentally different things
- Each has unique dimensions not shared with the other

## Primary Topic Rule (from Wikipedia:Primary topic)
When a term could refer to multiple things, the "primary topic" is the one that:
1. Is most likely sought by readers searching for that term
2. Has the most significance in the context of the wiki

For IndiaMART: the email SUBJECT is the strongest signal for primary topic.

## Redirect Rules (from Wikipedia:Redirect)
Create a redirect (alias) when:
- An alternate name, abbreviation, or spelling exists
- A topic was renamed
- Common misspellings should still find the right page

## Parent Hub Rules (from Wikipedia:Summary style)
Create a hub page when:
- 3+ pages form a family (variants of the same concept)
- Readers need an overview before diving into specifics
- The hub provides comparison/context that individual pages don't

Hub structure:
1. Overview paragraph explaining the family
2. Brief comparison table
3. Links to each individual page with 1-line summaries
